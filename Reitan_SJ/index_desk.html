Health Points Report

How SJ AB and Reitan Convenience motivated and engaged employees in health and team-building.

SJ is the national train operator in Sweden, serving the whole country, as well as parts of Denmark and Norway, with around 4000 employees. They are not newbies to our Challenges: this is their 9th Challenge together with us! And as always they did great!

Reitan Convenience is the second largest convenience company in Europe with more than 2300 stores and 14.000 employees. In Sweden, the company operates two brands that are part of everyone’s daily life: Pressbyrån and 7-Eleven. The company is part of Reitan Group, one of the biggest companies in Norway, employing 38000 people in Scandinavia and Baltic Area.

How do we measure Health Points?

We know that numbers and results matter. And to measure how our Challenge has affected the company, we have a 3 question survey that all participants answer before, during and after their Challenge. The questions are about how active you are, your physical form and general well-being. They seem simple, but they are a powerful tool to generate insights about how all the different teams, units and company as a whole are scoring.

How did the Challenges go?

The chart to the right is measurable insights how the companies developed during their challenge, when all the dimensions (health, physical activity and general well-being) are aggregated. Reitan started with an aggregated score of 5,79 and ended with 6.45 which is an amazing increase of 11.32%. As for SJ, they started with 6.18 and ended their Challenge with 6.99 giving them an impressive 13,9% growth.

What about the teams?

To the left we can see how the aggregated score is distributed among the teams in both companies. It is clear that the large majority have improved their overall score which is fantastic. One team improved it by almost 35%!

For SJ and Reitan it means employees that are healthier and more energized in their daily routine.

The team’s changes in activity level and well-being.

Here you can see how the two dimensions physical activity and general well-being progressed. It is clear that the majority of teams were more active and feeling much better than before, some improving by as much as 50%.

The negative teams matter

It is natural to celebrate only the positive results and those that increase motivation and engagement during the Challenge. However, the teams with a negative development are just as important to focus attention on.

Why? Because it is a clear indication that these teams might need a closer look. What is the cause of this negative development? Why are they scoring so differently to the rest? This is really important information for Human Resources and a clear call to action.
